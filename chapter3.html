WHAT FORCE THEM
TO LEAVE SYRIA?:

Our intention is to focus on why almost half of Syrian population had no choice but to leave the motherlands and which types of life threats have existed from the viewpoint of themselves. So we analyzed the data of syriatracker.crowdmap.com which reports several damages inflicted against civilians in and around Syria. Syria Tracker, website utilizing group intelligence, allows anonymous users to directly report cases of civilian deaths, massacre, genocide and also chemical warfare. You can find detailed process of analysis in the section of methodology.

6 TYPES OF CIVILIAN DAMAGE

BATTLE

refers to military conflicts among two or more groups. Syrian tracker involves not only in fatalities from combats but also in fatalities from bombings or shootings derived from battles.

SHELLING

: refers to assaults by artillery fire-forces that have been using by Assad regime in a most universal level. Though it is usually applied to battles with anti-regime forces, shelling cases have been also found in civilian areas.

AIR STRIKE

is an attack of aerial bombardment on a specific target using missile, bombing incidents by military aircrafts.

CHEMICAL WEAPONE

is a munition that uses chemicals formulated to inflict death or harm on human beings. It was June 2012 when the regime used chemical munition for the first time but the issue caught world-wide attention by August 2013.

BARREL BOMB

is made from a barrel-like metal container with high explosives and shrapnel. It is typically bombed by aircrafts but strictly forbidden by UN convention.

DIRECT ATTACK

includes all the other attacks not pointed out above. This terminology may include direct attacks against individual or groups such as sniping, execution and massacre.

Finding2: Correlation between type of attcks and the number of deaths and refugees

With a view to arranging influence on monthly death tolls and number of refugees by the frequency of 6 types of attacks, we tried to analyze result statistically. So to speak, Pearson's product-mement correlation was adapted as an algorism tool to connect the dots of interrelation between civilian assaults and the number of deaths or refugees. In this regards, the correlation becomes higher when the result stands near 1.

Primarily, the table below says the correlation between monthly fatalities and six types of assaults with absolute value of each attack being provided.

Followed by the table, shelling incidents (0.78) and air strike (0.61) show the highest value in correlation, while barrel bomb (0.049) and battle (0.16) represent the lowest relevance. It's why we chose to mark figure of total reports in order to consider possible influence which quantity and intention of attacks might have on the correlation itself. Let's see each figure: shelling incidents are the most reported with the highest correlation but; air strikes are less reported than battles with the second highest interrelation – it implies that air strikes could result in massive killings with minor number of assaults or maintain the higher intention on civilian areas as the very target.

In terms of number of refugees each month, it does not demonstrate direct correlation with frequency of attacks. It's because of the gap between the two of increasing refugees and deaths. However, when time axis be pushed forward by 5 months, the correlation is increased to 0.68, showing that there are 5 month-gap between civilian assaults and refugee crisis.

Finding3: Moving toward fresh perspective

The data used in the analysis process is from Syrian Violation Document Center with the cause of the deaths being recorded. Nevertheless, we tried to analyze the correlation between type of damage and death toll so as to make an assumption about the intention of attacks itself.

The cause of deaths by VDE does not specify additional reasons whether the casualty was due to attacks against local community or due to fresh attacks after the first battles between militants.

By making use of the category ‘battle’, however, it was also able to distinguish originally intended civilian attacks from secondly-led damage among battles. Looking at the data again, battles of 5,567 cases and its correlation with fatalities show considerably low degree of 0.16 but air strikes of 3,543 cases represent 0.61. In other words, though air strike has relative lower reports than battles, it causes almost quadrupled victims. Of course, deaths during battles can also not be justifiable but analytic process enables the viewers to see much more persons than expected dying owing to non-selective attacks, who may have zero relevance with civil war itself. The data once again confirms the cruelty of war.
