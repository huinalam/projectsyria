WHAT FORCE THEM
TO LEAVE SYRIA?:

Our intention is to focus on why almost half of Syrian population had no choice but to leave the motherlands and which types of life threats have existed from the viewpoint of themselves. So we analyzed the data of syriatracker.crowdmap.com which reports several damages inflicted against civilians in and around Syria. Syria Tracker, website utilizing group intelligence, allows anonymous users to directly report cases of civilian deaths, massacre, genocide and also chemical warfare. You can find detailed process of analysis in the section of methodology.

By extracted data, timeline says the type of civilian losses and its frequency, fatalities and number of refugees on a monthly and yearly basis. Hence, we intended to let our viewers find out relevance between each data by pointing out which groups are related to certain types of incidents and which sorts of incidents were occurred. From 2011 to April 2016, 7 chapters are dissected as of patterns of civil war and also major timeline cases.

DEFINITIONS OF 6 TYPES OF CIVILIAN DAMAGE USED IN ANALYSIS

BATTLE

refers to military conflicts among two or more groups. Syrian tracker involves not only in fatalities from combats but also in fatalities from bombings or shootings derived from battles.

SHELLING

: refers to assaults by artillery fire-forces that have been using by Assad regime in a most universal level. Though it is usually applied to battles with anti-regime forces, shelling cases have been also found in civilian areas.

AIR STRIKE

is an attack of aerial bombardment on a specific target using missile, bombing incidents by military aircrafts.

CHEMICAL WEAPONE

is a munition that uses chemicals formulated to inflict death or harm on human beings. It was June 2012 when the regime used chemical munition for the first time but the issue caught world-wide attention by August 2013.

BARREL BOMB

is made from a barrel-like metal container with high explosives and shrapnel. It is typically bombed by aircrafts but strictly forbidden by UN convention.

DIRECT ATTACK

includes all the other attacks not pointed out above. This terminology may include direct attacks against individual or groups such as sniping, execution and massacre.

3.Involvement of Islamic Power
(2012 Jan ~ 2013 Apr)

Summary

On 2012, several other factions around Middle East came to be incorporated. Notably, Kruds people who had been willing to gain independence from Syria established YPG, known as People’s Defence units succeeded occupying some northern area of the country, while Lebanese armed group of Hezbollah was deployed in battlefields as major supporting partner of the regime. From October of 2012, several battles between Assad’s and anti-regime forces were erupted in 7 cities including al-Quasayr, then another format of battles between YPG and rebel forces appeared in northern area in November. Obama administration gave permission of CIA training program for rebel forces in April, 2013.

4.Escalation of the War & born of ISIS
(2013 Jan ~ 2014 Jan)

Summary

The worsened inner split of al-Qaeda’s Syria branch resulted in the birth of ISIS in April. In the meantime, Assad regime killed 213 civilians by activating chemical weapons in August. Though Moscow pressured Damascus to give up the usage of chemical weapons and Syria accepted the proposal outwardly in September, continuous weapon uses have been observed. In the same month, rebel forces trained by CIA were deployed in al-Nusra, the Syrian branch of al-Qaeda. In first month of 2014, ISIS occupied Raqqa where government forces had controlled.

However, local media outlets including ‘Halab today’ has been distributing the videos relevant to their suspicions that Russian targets in Syria have been anti-regime forces-held regions and other civilian areas.
