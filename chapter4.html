THE NUMBER OF DEATHS & REFUGEES

ABOUT DATA USED

Death per a month

The Violation Docmentation Center provides daily report of death in Syria. We refine the data set into data set of monthly death to compare with the number of refugee per a month.

Refugee per a month

UNHCR provide data set of change of registered refugees. Date factor of this data set is so random and it also confined only middle east country. However, considering fact that about 98% of whole Syrian refugee stay in middle east area, we determined that this data set is enough vaild to confirm tendancy of refugees number by the time. Therefore, we refine the original data into monthly data of registered refugee.

Possible Bias

VDC is one of the rare data source where we can access list of death in Syria. However, they can miss some list of death where they can't reach to investiage, for example Raqqa, where Islamic State mainly occupied.

Data of registered refugee provided by UNHCR doesn't represent exact and immediate the number of refugee generated on each date. It rather represent the number of refugees that UNHRC confirm on each date. And there are several outlier points which are hard to be explained. For example, more than 500,000 refugees were recorded on Dec 2014, which is almost as twice as the highest records in the past.

THE MOST INTESIVE DAMAGES

One of the largest characteristics we have seen in timeline is the worst damage happened after June 2012 during a year. More than 1,400 shelling incidents were reported only in November 2011 with other types of air strikes and direct bombings were also publicized. This coincides with the right point when the highest fatalities occurred the figures delclared seem to have something to do with death tolls and direct attacks.

CORRELATION BETWEEN DEATH AND REFUGEE

With a view to arranging influence on monthly death tolls and number of refugees by the frequency of 6 types of attacks, we tried to analyze result statistically. So to speak, Pearson's product-mement correlation was adapted as an algorism tool to connect the dots of interrelation between civilian assaults and the number of deaths or refugees. In this regards, the correlation becomes higher when the result stands near 1.

In terms of number of refugees each month, it does not demonstrate direct correlation with frequency of attacks. It's because of the gap between the two of increasing refugees and deaths. However, when time axis be pushed forward by 5 months, the correlation is increased to 0.68, showing that there are 5 month-gap between civilian assaults and refugee crisis.
